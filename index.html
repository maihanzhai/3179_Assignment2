Education in The World

Education is a purposeful activity. It can spread knowledge or develop skills. At the same time, we can shape a better society through education to understand and respect rights, laws and regulations. In short, for every country, education is a top priority. Every country and the United Nations are working together to improve the quality and efficiency of education.

Literacy Rates in Different Countries

This map shows the literacy rate of each country at different times.

Among these countries, we can find that the majority of the world's countries have literacy rates above 50%. According to the map, there are still some countries in the African region where the literacy rate is around 30%.

Of these countries, in the 2000, Finland's national literacy rate reached 100%. Greenland had a 100% literacy rate in 2001. Norway, on the other hand, had a 100% literacy rate by 2011.

Literacy and Illiteracy Rates in The World in Different Years

According to my search on the Internet, the earliest literacy in the form of books appeared around 3,500-3,000 BC. But literacy was still limited by many things. It was not until the Middle Ages that people gradually had the opportunity to become literate because of the mass production of books.

This line graph shows the change in the world's illiteracy and literacy rates between 1800 and 2016.

According to the change of the line cahrt, we can see that the literacy rate has been increasing. However, the growth was relatively slow until 1930. It grew very quickly around 1930 because at that time, education was highly regarded by people.

Literacy and Illiteracy Rates in The World

The four donut charts are created from the above line chart. They show the percentage of literacy and illiteracy rates in the world for four different periods: 1820, 1900, 1950, and 2000.

The orange part inside the donut chart represents the world literacy population. Comparing these four donut charts, we can clearly see that the orange part has become more and more weighted as time goes on. This means that more and more people in the world are becoming literate.

The Percentage of Repeaters in Primary Education in Each Country

This bar chart represents the percentage of elementary school repeaters in different countries. The country of Burundi has the highest elementary school repetition rate of all the study subjects, with 24.25%. Which means that this country's education level is relatively backward.
